6-Bar Synthesis & Time Ratio Derivation

The chosen architecture is a 6-bar linkage comprising a primary 4-bar crank-rocker driving a secondary dyad attached to the output sector. The primary 4-bar determines the quick-return timing, while the secondary dyad maps the rocker's oscillation to the strictly vertical translation and rotation of the sector through positions A, B, and C.

Deriving the Quick-Return Geometry

Let the Time Ratio ($TR$) be defined as the ratio of the time of the forward stroke (slow) to the return stroke (fast). Given the input parameter $TR = 2$, we can determine the crank rotation angles for the forward stroke ($\alpha$) and the return stroke ($\beta$):

$$ TR = \frac{\alpha}{\beta} $$ $$ \alpha + \beta = 360^\circ $$

Substituting $TR$ into the equation:

$$ \beta \cdot TR + \beta = 360^\circ \implies \beta(TR + 1) = 360^\circ $$

The imbalance angle ($\delta$), which dictates the offset of the rocker pivots relative to the crank center, is calculated as: $\delta = |180^\circ - \beta|$. This $\delta$ forms the geometric basis for synthesizing the ground link and crank lengths of the primary 4-bar loop.
