Kinematic Velocity Analysis & Vector Polygons

The velocity analysis utilizes the relative velocity method, taking the time derivative of the position loops. The analysis is broken into two stages for the 6-bar linkage:

  1. Primary 4-Bar Loop: The velocity of the rocker pin ($V_B$) is related to the crank pin ($V_A$) by:
    V_B = V_A + V_{B/A}
  2. Secondary Dyad Loop: The absolute velocity of the output sector center ($V_{out}$) is constrained strictly vertically and relates to the rocker pin by:
    V_{out} = V_B + V_{out/B}

All absolute velocities originate from the stationary velocity pole ($o_v$). Relative velocities are perpendicular to their respective links, forming closed polygons that guarantee kinematic continuity.
