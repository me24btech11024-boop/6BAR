Kinematic Acceleration Analysis

Because we are utilizing a standard 4-bar crank-rocker driving a dyad (with no sliding joints on rotating links), the Coriolis component is absent. The acceleration vectors are built entirely from Normal (centripetal) and Tangential components:

  • Normal Acceleration ($A^n$): Directed along the link towards the center of rotation (magnitude: $V^2/R$).
  • Tangential Acceleration ($A^t$): Directed perpendicular to the link (magnitude: $\alpha \times R$).
  • Output Center ($A_{out}$): The acceleration of the 270° circular arc is constrained to a pure vertical vector.
