ME2220: Project Stage II

Problem Statement & Overview

The objective is to synthesize a 6-bar quick-return mechanism using revolute and prismatic joints. The mechanism must move a 270° circular sector (radius 5 cm) from orientation A to C through B.

  • Motion Path: The center of the object translates strictly vertically downwards (5 mm at Position B, 15 mm at Position C).
  • Rotation: The object exhibits a 180° rotation between positions A and C.
  • Timing: The mechanism must execute a quick-return motion, returning to position A twice as fast as the forward stroke (Time Ratio = 2).

Mechanism Architecture: Based on the desired output, the model utilizes a 6-bar linkage. A primary four-bar crank-rocker loop acts as the time-ratio driver, while a secondary dyad connected to the slider ensures pure vertical translation and the required rotational output of the sector.

Use the left control panel to adjust the numerical dimensions. The variables are saved globally and will update the kinematic analysis and animation on the subsequent tabs.
